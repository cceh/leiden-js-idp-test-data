            ((1))Year twenty two of Imperator Caesar Titus Aelius Hadrianus Anto[n]inus Augustus Pius, 
            Tybi 10, through the Gymnasium bank of Sarapion. ((5))Sto[t]oetis, son of Stotoetis, the son 
            of Stotoetis, from the village of Soknopaiou Nesos, (acknowledges) to A[m]mo[n]ios, son of Horigenes, 
            the son of Herakleides, of the <S[o]sicosmian tribe and of the Althaean 
                deme=SosikosmioshokaiAlthaieus>, ((9))through his 
            <agent=phrontistes> Pan[e]phremmis, son of Stabous, the son of Satabous, 
            from the village of Soknopaiou Nesos about 30 years old, with a scar on the first finger of his left 
            hand, ((12))that he has sold to the said Stotoetis a female camel, branded on its right haunch 
            ~|I Ny Eta|~la , which the said Stotoetis received and will register for the 
            <registration of beasts=tonthremmatonapographe> of the present year 
            ((18))and the said Panephremmis has received the remaining one hundred 
            <silver drachmas=touargurioudrachmai> of the price and will assure that 
            Ammonios consents to the sale and <will guarantee (the sale) with every 
                guarantee=bebaioseinpasebebaiosei>. ((23))Herakleides, son of 
            Isidoros, <:wrote|BGU:BL I 45|:> <:(the following 
                receipt)|:Cf. Preisigke, Girowesen, p. 359|:> on his behalf, because he says that 
            he (~|sc.|~la  Ammonios) does not know letters. ((25))(m. 2) A[mm]onios, son of Horigenes, 
            the son of Herakleides, of the <Sosicosmian tribe and of the Althaean 
                deme=SosikosmioshokaiAlthaieus>, through his 
            <agent=phrontistes> Panephremm[i]s, I have sold a female camel, of which

            (((34)))I, Herakleides, son of Isido[ros], wrote on his behalf because he is illiterate.
