                  ((1)) The 11th year of the Emperor Caesar Trajanus Hadrianus Augustus, the 16th
                         of the month Sebastus or Thoth, at Theadelphia in the division of Themistes of the ((5)) Arsinoite
                         nome. Mystharion, son of Heron, aged about 40 years, having a scar on the little finger
                         of the left hand, agrees that he has sold to Satabous, son of Pekusis, aged about 30 years,
                         having a scar ((10)) on the little finger of the left hand, a female mouse-coloured donkey,
                         shedding its first teeth, just as it is irrevocably, and that Mystharion has received from
                         Satabous ((15)) the price agreed upon between them, 56 drachmae of silver, directly from hand
                         to hand out of his house, and that he will guarantee the sale with every guarantee.
                ((20)) (Signature of Mystharion written for him by Heraclides, and docket of the record office at Theadelphia.)
